## Definition
RS = Right shoulder, LS = Left shoulder
- X = norm(RS - LS)
- Y = norm(Shoulder - hip)
- Z = cross(X, Y)

Re-orthogonalize:
- Y = cross(Z, X)

This means that:
- +X = Person's right
- +Y = Up
- +Z = Forward, out of chest

## Definition of angle conventions

### Shoulder flexion
Flexion is positiove when the arm moves toward +Z
So:
- Arm forward -> positive (+90 grad)
- Arm backward -> negative
- Arm down = 0 deg

### Shoulder abduction
Abduction is positive when the arm moves toward +X
So:
- Arm out to the side -> positive
- Across the body -> negative (not possible with physical arm)

### Shoulder internal rotation
Rotiation around the upper arm axis
Estimated with IK.

### Elbow flexion
Flexion is positive when the angle between upper arm and forearm decreases
